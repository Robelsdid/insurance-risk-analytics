# Insurance Terminology Glossary

## Key Insurance Terms for Risk Analytics

### General Insurance Terms

1. **Premium**: The amount paid by the policyholder to the insurance company for coverage.

2. **Claim**: A formal request by a policyholder to an insurance company for coverage or compensation for a covered loss or policy event.

3. **Deductible**: The amount the policyholder must pay out-of-pocket before the insurance coverage kicks in.

4. **Policy**: A contract between the insurer and the policyholder that outlines the terms and conditions of insurance coverage.

5. **Underwriting**: The process by which an insurer determines whether to accept a risk and what premium to charge.

6. **Actuary**: A professional who analyzes the financial consequences of risk using mathematics, statistics, and financial theory.

7. **Risk Assessment**: The process of evaluating potential risks to determine the likelihood and impact of loss.

8. **Coverage**: The protection provided by an insurance policy, detailing what the insurer will and will not pay for.

9. **Endorsement**: A written amendment attached to an insurance policy that alters the policy's coverage, terms, or conditions.

10. **Exclusion**: Specific conditions, perils, or circumstances listed in the policy for which the insurer will not provide coverage.

### Car Insurance Specific Terms

11. **Comprehensive Coverage**: Insurance that covers damage to your vehicle not caused by a collision, such as theft, vandalism, or natural disasters.

12. **Collision Coverage**: Insurance that covers damage to your vehicle resulting from a collision with another vehicle or object.

13. **Liability Coverage**: Insurance that covers bodily injury and property damage you cause to others in an accident.

14. **No-Fault Insurance**: A system where each driver's insurance company pays for their medical expenses regardless of who caused the accident.

15. **Personal Injury Protection (PIP)**: Coverage for medical expenses and, in some cases, lost wages and other damages.

16. **Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist Coverage**: Protection when an at-fault driver has insufficient or no insurance.

17. **Gap Insurance**: Coverage that pays the difference between the actual cash value of a vehicle and the amount still owed on a loan or lease.

18. **Depreciation**: The decrease in the value of a vehicle over time due to wear and tear, age, and market conditions.

19. **Excess**: Similar to a deductible, the amount the policyholder must pay toward a claim before the insurer pays.

20. **Third-Party Insurance**: Coverage for damage or injury you cause to someone else or their property.

### Risk and Analytics Terms

21. **Risk Pool**: A group of individuals or entities with similar risk profiles grouped together for insurance purposes.

22. **Loss Ratio**: The ratio of total losses incurred in claims plus adjustment expenses divided by the total premiums earned.

23. **Combined Ratio**: The sum of the loss ratio and expense ratio, used to measure underwriting profitability.

24. **Frequency**: The number of claims per exposure unit (e.g., per policy or per car-year).

25. **Severity**: The average cost per claim.

26. **Expected Loss**: The anticipated amount of loss based on historical data and risk factors.

27. **Risk Factor**: A characteristic associated with an increased likelihood of loss.

28. **Exposure**: The vulnerability to loss, often measured in units such as car-years.

29. **Experience Rating**: A method of determining premiums based on the insured's past loss experience.

30. **Credibility**: A measure of the predictive value of a particular set of data.

### Financial and Business Terms

31. **Reserve**: Funds set aside by an insurer to pay future claims.

32. **Reinsurance**: Insurance purchased by an insurer from another insurer to manage risk and protect against large losses.

33. **Retention**: The amount of risk retained by an insured or an insurer after reinsurance.

34. **Margin**: The difference between premiums collected and claims paid, representing potential profit.

35. **Acquisition Cost**: The expense associated with obtaining new business, including marketing and commissions.

36. **Lapse Rate**: The percentage of policies that are not renewed.

37. **Persistency**: The rate at which policies remain in force, the opposite of lapse rate.

38. **Renewal Rate**: The percentage of policies that are renewed at the end of their term.

39. **Cross-Selling**: The practice of selling additional products to existing customers.

40. **Upselling**: The practice of encouraging customers to purchase a higher-end product or add-on services.

### Statistical and Modeling Terms

41. **Predictive Modeling**: Using statistical techniques to predict future behavior or outcomes.

42. **Segmentation**: Dividing a population into distinct groups based on shared characteristics.

43. **Correlation**: A statistical measure that expresses the extent to which two variables are linearly related.

44. **Regression Analysis**: A statistical method used to estimate relationships between variables.

45. **Classification**: A machine learning technique that assigns items into categories.

46. **Clustering**: A technique that groups similar data points together based on certain features.

47. **Feature Engineering**: The process of selecting and transforming variables for a predictive model.

48. **Overfitting**: When a model learns the training data too well, including its noise and outliers.

49. **Validation**: The process of evaluating a model's performance using data not used in training.

50. **Confidence Interval**: A range of values that is likely to contain the true value of a parameter.
